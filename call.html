The Making of Cross-Device Experiences:

A Hands-on Workshop

Call for Proposals

User experience designers increasingly need to consider human activities that span multiple devices, as recent studies have shown that people use or attempted to use several devices together to carry out everyday tasks. Yet, there are tremendous challenges when it comes to designing cross-device experiences that are useful, usable, and enjoyable. This workshop will focus on the following themes:

  • Maintaining UI consistency across-platforms vs. within platforms
  • Anticipating and designing for impromptu cross-device usage
  • Directing the userâ€™s attention in a cross-device workflow
  • Creating experiences that can move across or be dynamically split between devices
  • Leveraging the spatial layout of devices as a resource for interaction design
  • Prototyping cross-device interactions in a way that fits existing workflows and user testing requirements
  • Closing the gap between design and engineering in building cross-device experiences

We invite contributions from members of the design and research communities who are wrestling with these issues. The workshop will be hands-on; we will collaboratively design, prototype, and critique cross-device experiences and the process of making them.

This workshop does not require a position paper. Instead, interested applicants should submit a statement of interest describing their background, expertise, area of interest, and if applicable tools/projects they wish to showcase or continue working on in the workshop. In addition, applicants are strongly encouraged to submit a video to supplement their statement. Accepted applicants are required to register for both the workshop and at least one day of the conference.
